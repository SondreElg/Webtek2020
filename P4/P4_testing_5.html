Tester audience

For testing, we performed both surveying with a web form, and live user testing. A Google Form was sent to members of Anime NTNU and other groups of interest. For user testing, the group members contacted friends and acquaintances, gave them some questions and tasks, and observed their responses. In total, 13 responses from the webform were submitted, and 8 live tests were performed.

Considering that Anime NTNU is a student organization, the target audience consists mainly of students. This has been reflected in our testing procedure, considering that all the people participating in live testing and the majority of webform submitters were students.

Testing Platforms

We were able to run tests with a variety of platforms and web browsers. The platforms covered were Windows 10, Mac, and mobile. Among the web browsers used were Google Chrome, Microsoft Edge, Firefox, Safari, and Vivaldi.

Observed testing

In addition to the digital schema we sent out, we conducted eight live tests where we observed how a test person interacted with the website. The test person chose their unit and browser of preference and tested different features and functionalities available on the website. As we live tested quite a lot of people, we would like to highlight the testing procedure and feedback received from two of the testers. At the end of this section, we will briefly state the overall issues, opinions and feedback received from all the live tests.

The tasks performed in the live testing were the following:

  1. How can you contact Anime NTNU?
  2. Find information about the first upcoming screening.
  3. What anime are being watched this semester?
  4. When is the last screening of the semester?
  5. What anime were being watched in the fall of 2013?
  6. Vote for which anime you would like to watch.
  7. What is Sayonara Zetsubou Sensei about?
  8. What is Anime NTNU?
  9. Create a user.
  10. How can you attend screenings?

Test person using a Windows desktop computer with Google Chrome

This person was able to navigate to the desired elements very quickly. They used the navigation bar extensively. They immediately understand how to arrange the elements in the voting list.

They explained that they found it easy to find information, they found the voting page practical, and they thought it was positive that information about all upcoming screenings was published. However, they found it impractical that they had to join the Discord server to find more information. They were uncertain in which order the episodes were shown. They also wanted the ability to show voting results.

Test person using a Mac with Safari

This user was very quick to use the navbar and always found the correct pages for each question after a single click. There was some confusion regarding the content on some of the pages and how to interact with it though.

When they were tasked to find an old news post on the newsfeed page, they clicked the “Show all news”-button a total of 4 times before they realized all news had been loaded in. Furthermore, when tasked with finding what anime are being screened this semester, they went through both “Screenings” and “Newsfeed” before concluding with “It’s different”, not being certain what anime was being screened throughout the semester.

After the tasks they commented a lot on the overall design of the website, describing it as straightforward to use and minimalistic. They also thought it was nice how it was clear what elements were clickable. On the other hand, they found it confusing that the list on the voting page reset after submitting their votes, suggesting that they should stay as submitted.

General experiences from testing

Some of the live tests uncovered that how to vote was unclear. One user tried clicking an element and awaiting response, and then pressing the submit button and clicking ‘OK’ on the subsequent prompt. They performed these actions for a few repetitions, before finally reading the textual instructions on the top of the page and thereby discovering that the elements could be dragged and dropped.

Overall, among the eight live testers, there were a few recurring issues. In particular, finding information on how to attend a screening seemed to be unclear. Several users navigated to the screenings page, containing the time and location of the screenings, but it was unclear that this was the information they needed. They tried clicking the popup containing only the summary, following by closing the popup and navigating elsewhere to the site. It is apparent that this information could be relayed clearer, for example via a small text description on the front page and/or the screenings page.

Feedback Summary

Positive feedback

Users seemed to agree in large that the front page was helpful. Finding information about screenings and news seemed to not be an issue for the most part. Most users commented positively on ease of navigation. Praise was also given to the synopsis popups, and pointed out that such a solution was much better than being navigated to a separate page.

Constructive feedback

Opinions about the overall design were largely differing. Some users commented that the design was easy on the eyes and left an appropriate amount of spacing between elements. Others said they did not like the colors, and that the navigation bar was too small on a phone. We also received feedback that the sign up button was too different compared to the rest of the menu items.

As discussed in the section about observed testing, the process of voting was criticized. Both in the live testing and the survey, we received feedback that finding out how to vote was not trivial. One user also noted the lack of voting results.

Response to feedback

Some of the feedback collected was used to further develop the website before the deadline, and these changes have been mentioned in P3. For example, many users struggled finding information about which anime is being screened this semester. The screenings page was updated to list this explicitly. We also changed the signup button to be more stylistically similar to the rest of the menu items.

As described earlier, there seemed to be a lack of clarity in some cases regarding how to vote for which anime a user wants to watch. This problem could be (partially or completely) solved with the following measures:

  • Create a visual indication, like an icon for every list element, indicating that the elements can be dragged
  • Shorten the text explanation, or add a shorter but physically larger element of text saying for example “Drag and drop to vote”
  • After voting, the user should be informed that their votes have been submitted

In addition, the voting page was known not to work in Firefox and on touch devices. This is reflected in the responses from the corresponding question on the survey.

There were many who commented on how our site only provided Discord as a means to contact Anime NTNU. Although we wanted to address this issue, Anime NTNU as an organization has no other means of contacting them, so there was little we could do.

Self-reflection

Maja

During this project I have learned the importance of good communication and teamwork, especially considering the current circumstances. Due to all the group members having different time schedules in addition to the ongoing pandemic, we have been relying a lot on digital platforms for communication and sharing work. We have for example been using Git and Github to work on the same project, making it easy and efficient to get the project up-to-date. This was mainly possible due to the team members prior knowledge in these technologies and platforms, however, I still do not think it would have been easy had we not been constantly requesting updates and feedback from each other.

I have also gained more experience using Javascript during this project. I have used Javascript before, but I have never made a drag-and-drop poll like I did for the voting page. This was a challenge in itself, and I realised, after making it, that we could have looked at other options for the voting system, as the one I implemented into the website is not functional on touch screens.

Lastly, I have learned a lot about working in a project with a real client. Having worked with a real client made me realise the importance of thinking about what you are making and who you are making it for. This was made apparent after receiving feedback from the client in the design process as well as when implementing the actual features on the website.

Tobias

I have learned how-to plan a website and the importance of properly planning a website before production. There are many aspects of producing a website that I never would’ve thought of if they were not specified in the project deliverables. By specifying many of the production stages and elements of a webpage, the production of said elements also became much easier. I have also seen the importance of user testing, and how the general consensus of the testers may differ from our own expectations and opinions. In the future I would be inclined to include user testing on more projects, both personal and other assignments.

Aleksander

I have seen how easy it is to overlook implementation details in the planning phase. During development, in many cases, we needed to create more files and functions than what we initially imagined. For example, to make the frontpage elements display nicely on narrow screens, both media queries and flexboxes were utilized. Additionally, we seemed to underestimate what functionality would be useful. For instance, the amount of news posts from Anime NTNU’s existing website was quite large, causing us to implement a feature for showing only a subset of the posts. I believe this goes to show how important it is to use a good amount of time for planning, to make time and workload estimates as accurate as possible.
